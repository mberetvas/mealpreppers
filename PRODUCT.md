# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Families and households coordinating weekly meal preparation. They plan together, cook in batches, and shop from a unified list. Context is typically a weekend planning session (phone or laptop at the kitchen table) followed by a grocery run.

## Product Purpose
Help families plan a week menu and automatically generate a grocery list from that menu. Success looks like: planning next week's meals takes minutes, not guesswork, and the shopping list is always complete and accurate.

## Brand Personality
Warm, curated, editorial. The "Culinary Atelier" voice treats home cooking as a craft worth celebrating, not a chore to optimize. Friendly authority without being precious.

## Anti-references
- Industrial ERP systems: sterile grids, data-dump tables, zero personality, overwhelms with options
- Generic meal-kit subscription apps (HelloFresh/Marley Spoon style): stock-photo heavy, gamified upsell patterns, corporate-warm
- Cluttered recipe aggregators: ad-choked, competing CTAs, no clear workflow

## Design Principles
1. **Flow over features** — the path from "what do we eat?" to "here's the shopping list" should feel like one continuous motion, not separate screens bolted together.
2. **Household, not user** — design for shared context. Multiple people contribute recipes, adjust portions, and check off groceries.
3. **Calm confidence** — the interface should feel like it already knows what you need next. No tutorial modals, no overwhelm.
4. **Ingredients first** — food is the content. Photography, ingredient names, and quantities deserve visual priority over UI chrome.
5. **Sunday-proof** — works under time pressure with messy hands and divided attention. Touch targets, clear hierarchy, forgiving interactions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- WCAG AA compliance (contrast, focus states, semantic markup)
- Touch-friendly targets (min 44px) for kitchen/mobile use
- Reduced-motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Legible at arm's length (planning sessions often happen with the phone propped up)
